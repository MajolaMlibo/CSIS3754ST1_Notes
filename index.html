3. Working with DataFrames

Inspection & Exploration

  • df.head(n): View first n rows.
  • df.shape: Returns dimensions (rows, columns).
  • df.info(): Index, Datatype, and Memory info.
  • df.describe(): Summary statistics for numerical columns.
  • df.value_counts(): Count unique occurrences.

Selection & Slicing

  • By Label (.loc): mydata.loc["RowLabel", "ColLabel"].
  • By Position (.iloc): mydata.iloc[2, 0] (3rd row, 1st col).
  • Boolean Filter: (df["rev"] > 100) & (df["prof"] < 0).

4. Data Cleaning

  • df.isnull(): Checks for null values.
  • df.dropna(): Drops rows with null values.
  • df.fillna(x): Replaces nulls with x.
  • s.astype(float): Converts Series to float.
  • df.rename(columns={'old':'new'}): Selective renaming.

5. Statistics & Visualization

Statistics

  • df.mean(): Mean of columns.
  • df.corr(): Pearson's r correlation matrix.
  • df.median(): Median of columns.

Plots

  • Series.plot.hist(): Histogram.
  • Series.plot.bar(): Vertical bar plot.
  • df.plot.scatter(x='c1', y='c2'): Scatter plot.
